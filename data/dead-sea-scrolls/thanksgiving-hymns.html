The Thanksgiving Hymns (1QH)

Hebrew: Hodayot - Songs of Praise and Thanksgiving

Found in Cave 1 at Qumran - One of the original seven scrolls

Hymn 1 - The Teacher's Thanksgiving

I thank You, O Lord,
for You have redeemed my soul from the Pit,
and from the Sheol of Abaddon
You have raised me up to everlasting height.

I walk on limitless level ground,
and I know there is hope for him
whom You have shaped from dust
for the everlasting Council.

You have cleansed a perverse spirit of great sin
that it may stand with the host of the Holy Ones,
and that it may enter into community
with the congregation of the Sons of Heaven.

Hymn 2 - Confession of Human Weakness

I thank You, O Lord,
for You have enlightened me through Your truth.
In Your marvelous mysteries,
and in Your loving-kindness to a man of sin,
and in the abundance of Your mercy to a perverse heart.

What is he, man, that is but earth?
Kneaded from water, his foundation is guilty sin,
shameful nakedness, a source of uncleanness,
over which a spirit of perversity rules.

If he acts wickedly,
he becomes an everlasting sign,
a portent for the generations,
an object of horror to all flesh.

Hymn 3 - The Teacher's Persecution

I thank You, O Lord,
for You have placed my soul in the bundle of the living,
and have hedged me about
against all the snares of the Pit.

Violent men have sought after my life
because I have clung to Your Covenant.
For they, an assembly of deceit,
and a horde of Belial,
know not that my stand is from You.

You have saved my life from the Pit,
and have raised my horn on high.
I shine with a sevenfold light,
in the Council which You have established for Your glory.

Hymn 4 - The Suffering Servant

I thank You, O Lord,
for You have upheld me by Your strength.
You have shed Your Holy Spirit upon me
that I may not stumble.

You have strengthened me
before the battles of wickedness,
and during all their disasters
You have not permitted that fear
should cause me to desert Your Covenant.

You have set me like a strong tower,
like a high wall;
You have established my building upon rock,
eternal foundations serve as my ground,
and all my walls are a tested wall which cannot be shaken.

Hymn 5 - Creation and Knowledge

I thank You, O Lord,
for You have illumined my face by Your Covenant.
I seek You,
and as sure as the dawn
You appear to me.

Teachers of lies have smoothed Your people with words,
and false prophets have led them astray;
they perish without understanding
for their works are in folly.

But I, through You, have become
like a spring of flowing water,
like an irrigation channel through a garden,
like rain upon grass,
and like showers upon what grows.

Hymn 6 - The Light of Knowledge

I thank You, O Lord,
for You have placed me at a source of streams in dry ground,
a spring of water in a land of drought,
a well-watered garden,
a planting of cypress, pine and cedar together for Your glory.

Trees of life are hidden
among all trees by the waters,
they shall put forth a shoot of the everlasting planting.
Before they flower they send out roots to the stream.

Its trunk is open to the living waters,
and it becomes an everlasting spring.
On its leafy branches every beast of the forest feeds,
its trunk is trodden by all who pass,
and its branches by all winged birds.

Hymn 7 - The Mystery of Knowledge

I thank You, O Lord,
for You have given me understanding of Your truth
and have made me know Your wonderful secrets.
Your loving-kindness is upon a man of perversity
and the abundance of Your mercy is on a crooked heart.

Where could I find one like You?
What creature of clay can do great things?
From sinful birth and from the womb
he is given over to guilty iniquity.

Righteousness, I know, is not of man,
nor is perfection of way of a son of man;
to the Most High God belong all righteous deeds.

Hymn 8 - The Community of the Covenant

I thank You, O Lord,
for You have set my soul in life
and have given me knowledge of Your testimonies.
I will praise Your Name among those who fear You.

With songs of thanksgiving I will open my mouth,
and my tongue shall ever recite
both Your righteousness and the deeds of Your hands,
for You have done wonders.

You have dealt graciously with the poor one
and have brought him into Your purifying waters for judgment.
You have strengthened his loins to stand
before the army of the wicked.

Hymn 9 - Final Thanksgiving

Blessed are You, O Lord,
for You have not abandoned the orphan
or despised the poor.
Your might is without bound
and Your glory is without measure.

Wonderful Counselors are among Your glory;
but man, in his lot of vileness,
and his inheritance of guilt—
what can he say?
What can he answer before You?

You are the Prince of gods
and King of the glorious ones,
Lord of every spirit
and Ruler of every creature.
Without You nothing is done,
and nothing is known without Your will.

There is no one beside You,
and no one approaches You in strength.
Nothing compares with Your glory,
and Your might—there is no price.
