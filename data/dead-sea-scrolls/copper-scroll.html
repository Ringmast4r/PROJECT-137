The Copper Scroll (3Q15)

An ancient treasure map engraved on copper sheets

Found in Cave 3 at Qumran in 1952

Introduction

Unlike the other Dead Sea Scrolls written on parchment or papyrus, the Copper Scroll is engraved on copper sheets. It lists approximately 64 locations where gold, silver, and other treasures are supposedly hidden. The total treasure would be worth billions today. Whether these treasures actually exist or are symbolic remains debated.

Column I

1. In the fortress which is in the Valley of Achor, forty cubits under the steps entering to the east: a money chest and its contents, of a weight of seventeen talents.

2. In the sepulchral monument, in the third course: 100 bars of gold.

3. In the great cistern which is in the court of the peristyle, in a socket in the bottom of it, hidden in a hole opposite the upper opening: nine hundred talents.

Column II

4. In the hill of Kohlit, tithe vessels of the Lord of the Nations and sacred vestments. Total of the tithes: seventeen talents.

5. At the head of the water conduit at the edge of the place which is called Sekakah, buried at a depth of four cubits: forty-two talents.

6. In the fissure which is in the narrow passage going northward: vessels of offering, along with their inventory.

Column III

7. In the plastered cistern which has conduits: a large amount in bars, as far as the threshold.

8. In the tomb which is in the wadi of Kippa, from the house of Hakoz, going from east to west: six cubits from the north, there are buried there sixty talents of silver.

9. In the water tank of the valley, at its western side: a pitcher of oil, and under it twenty-two talents of silver.

Column IV

10. In the large drain of the sepulchral chamber, at the place where it empties into the cavity: nine talents.

11. At the place of the two pools, at the entrance to the smaller pool: vessels of offering and scrolls, along with their inventory.

12. In the second enclosure, in the underground passage looking east: seven talents of silver.

Column V

13. In the cistern opposite the east gate, at a distance of nineteen cubits: vessels of silver and gold offering vessels, and silver. The total amount: six hundred talents.

14. In the pit which is north of the mouth of the gorge of Beth Tamar: all the items of the oil house, and offering vessels.

15. In the vault which is in the esplanade: twenty-two talents.

Column VI

16. In the pond next to the conduit, at the edge of its rim: four hundred talents.

17. In the cave of the old washer's house, at the third platform: sixty-five bars of gold.

18. In the underground chamber which is in the court, at the great threshold in the corner facing northwest: fourteen vessels of offering and scrolls, with their inventory.

Column VII

19. At Horon, in the land of the king, in the palace: twelve talents.

20. In the memorial mound of the channel of Koziba: silver and gold offerings.

21. In the Queen's residence, on the west side, buried at a depth of twelve cubits: twenty-seven talents.

Column VIII

22. In the tomb of the sons of the third estate, at the mouth of the stone fence: 100 bars of gold.

23. In the great cistern which is in the colonnaded court, in its corner, buried at a depth of two cubits: an amount of silver.

24. At the pool of Solomon, at the northwest corner of the ridge of the aqueduct: two hundred talents of silver.

Column IX

25. In the cave that is in the ridge of the cistern: twenty-three talents.

26. In the tomb of Absalom, in the cave of the valley of Kidron: eighty talents.

27. In the reservoir underneath the great opening, at the east side: seventy talents of silver in wooden containers.

Column X

28. At the mouth of the spring of Koziba: silver vessels, and gold vessels, and offering vessels. The total amount: six hundred and nine.

29. In the cave of the pillar with two openings, looking east, at the north entrance, buried three cubits: there is a sealed pitcher, and in it one scroll, under it forty-two talents.

Column XI

30. In the underground passage which is in the Beth Kerem area, going from south to north, at the edge of the burial: 100 talents of gold.

31. At the monument, at the third row of stones: one hundred gold bars.

Column XII - Final Entry

32. In the pit near the water outlet, at its north side, buried at a depth of three cubits: a sealed jar, and in it one book, and under it forty-two talents.

A copy of this inventory is in another location, with its explanation and its measurements, and the complete reckoning of everything, one by one.

The Copper Scroll (3Q15) - Qumran Cave 3 - Total treasures listed: approximately 4,600 talents of gold and silver
